## Overview

Riva Boston is the pre-launch site for a boutique 32-home development in one of
Boston's best-positioned neighborhoods. At this stage the goal isn't to sell a
specific unit — it's to build a waitlist: communicate the vision, the location,
and the quality of the homes, and convert early interest into a list of warm
leads the sales team can nurture toward launch.

## Scope

A focused early-access marketing site: an immersive hero, a neighborhood and
location story built around the development's positioning, design and finish
highlights, and a prominent sign-up flow for early updates and private
invitations. Everything funnels toward lead capture rather than transactional
detail, which comes later in the sales cycle.

## Role

Frontend engineer on the build. I assembled the site from the team's shared
component library, themed it to the development's brand, and implemented the
early-access capture flow and its email delivery.

## Stack

- **Next.js + TypeScript** — a fast, statically-rendered marketing site built
  for search and campaign traffic.
- **Tailwind CSS** — per-site theming via CSS variables over shared components.
- **Motion** — section reveals and transitions, all degrading gracefully under
  `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **Mapbox GL** — an interactive map framing the neighborhood and its
  connectivity, the development's core selling point.
- **React Hook Form + Zod** — a validated early-access form with typed input.
- **Resend** — transactional delivery of sign-ups to the sales team.

## Challenges

A pre-launch site has to sell a promise, not a product — there are no finished
units to photograph, so the work is in storytelling, pacing, and making the
location and design intent feel tangible. The conversion goal also shifts the
whole layout toward a single clear action — joining the early-access list —
without making the page feel like a pop-up.

## Outcomes

A live early-access site that turns the development's positioning into a growing
list of warm leads ahead of launch — delivered quickly on a shared system and
themed to read as its own brand.
