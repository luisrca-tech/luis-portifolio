## Overview

63 West is the marketing site for a single ultra-luxury oceanfront estate. When
the entire product is one address, the website _is_ the showroom: it has to
carry the craftsmanship, the setting, and the lifestyle in a way that justifies
the asking price before a buyer ever visits. The site leans on full-bleed
imagery, restrained motion, and a calm editorial pace to sell the feeling of the
place.

## Scope

A complete single-property marketing experience: an immersive hero, a residence
narrative, amenity and lifestyle sections, an image gallery, a Mapbox-driven
neighborhood and location view, and an inquiry flow that routes interested
buyers and brokers to the listing team by email. The whole site is built for
SEO and social sharing, since most traffic arrives from search and paid
campaigns.

## Role

Frontend engineer on the build. I assembled the site from the team's shared
component library, wired its content, tuned the motion and responsive layout,
and implemented the inquiry form and its email delivery.

## Stack

- **Next.js + TypeScript** — a statically-rendered, fully typed marketing site
  optimized for fast first paint and search visibility.
- **Tailwind CSS** — per-site theming through CSS variables, so the shared
  components take on this property's palette and typography without forking.
- **Motion** — reveal and parallax effects kept deliberately subtle to match a
  luxury tone, all degrading under `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **Mapbox GL** — an interactive location and neighborhood map highlighting the
  coastline and points of interest.
- **React Hook Form + Zod** — a validated inquiry form with typed input shared
  between client and server.
- **Resend** — transactional delivery of inquiries to the listing team.

## Challenges

The hard part of a single-property site is emotional rather than technical:
every section has to earn attention without ever feeling like a brochure. That
meant pacing the scroll, holding the motion back, and making the imagery the
loudest thing on the page while keeping it fast on mobile. Reusing a shared
component system without making the site look templated took deliberate theming
and layout work.

## Outcomes

A live, polished marketing site that presents the estate as a destination and
turns search and campaign traffic into qualified inquiries — produced quickly by
building on a shared system rather than from scratch, while still reading as
bespoke.
