## Overview

The Elle Group is a real-estate investment brokerage, not a single property, so
its site has a different job than a listing page: it has to build credibility.
The brand rests on the founder's story and a track record of helping clients
build wealth through real estate, and the site is structured to convey that
authority and turn visitors into advisory leads.

## Scope

A content-driven brand site: a hero and value proposition, a founder/"who we
are" narrative, a services overview, a featured-properties section, client
testimonials, a newsletter sign-up, and a blog of investing and market-insight
articles. The mix is deliberate — editorial content for trust and SEO, clear
service framing, and contact paths for conversion.

## Role

Frontend engineer on the build. I assembled the site from the team's shared
component system, adapted it to a content-and-brand layout rather than a
single-listing one, and wired the newsletter and inquiry flows.

## Stack

- **Next.js + TypeScript** — a statically-rendered site with a blog, tuned for
  search visibility on investing and market topics.
- **Tailwind CSS** — CSS-variable theming so the shared components carry the
  brokerage's brand.
- **Motion** — restrained reveals and transitions honoring
  `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **React Hook Form + Zod** — validated newsletter and inquiry forms with typed
  input.
- **Resend** — transactional delivery of inquiries and sign-ups.

## Challenges

Most sites in this portfolio sell a place; this one sells trust in a person and
a strategy. That shifted the work toward editorial structure — a readable blog,
a credible services narrative, and testimonial and founder sections — while
still reusing a component system designed for property listings. Bending that
system into a content brand site without losing its polish was the main effort.

## Outcomes

A live brand site that positions the brokerage as a credible investment partner
and routes readers from blog and service pages into the newsletter and advisory
funnel — built efficiently on the same shared system that powers the listing
sites.
